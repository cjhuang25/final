Reading Responses for Week 2 - Ulises Carrión - Selections from Second Thoughts

× In the reading, Carrión provides a juxtaposition of new art and old art. Something that stood out to me is "Plagiarism is the starting point of the creative activity in the new art." In society, the general consensus is that plagiarism is not beneficial to learning and prohibits creativity from individuals. Therefore, in many settings such as classrooms, plagiarism is prohibited and abstained from. What's interesting is that, the writer of this reading suggests that innovation sometimes derives from some sort of plagiarism -- inspiration from others' work. It also made me think that institutional and societal standards can sometimes prohibit inventions.

Reading Responses for Week 15 - Wendy Hui Kyong Chun - On Software or the Persistence of Visual Knowledge

× This reading discusses the concept of software and its relationship with transparency and visibility. The author argues that the conflation of information with transparency is at odds with the actual operations of computation, which require the use of glass to send and receive light pulses to re-create the referent. The current emphasis on transparency in product design and political and scholarly discourse is a compensatory gesture. Software perpetuates certain notions of seeing as knowing, of reading and readability, by mimicking both ideology and ideology critique, creating an invisible system of visibility. From this reading, I learned that it's imperative to engage media with software critically, examining the limitations of transcoding.

Reading Responses for Week 8 - Bojana Coklyat & Shannon Finnegan - Selections from Alt-Text as Poetry Workbook

× This piece of reading describes the use of al text and its importance in many fields, especially arts. People who can't see or have limited visions are often prevented from accessing and enjoying artworks; however, al text, a description of the image embedded in the code, helps people visualize the image. When writing al-texts, the writer should also keep in mind that the writing could contain subjectivity and personal interpretation. It's difficult to describe images, especially artworks, without adding in an element of your own opinion, but we should stay away from doing that as much as possible.

Reading Responses for Week 11 - Ramsey Nasser - Selections from Decolonising the Digital

× This reading provides insights into making design and the creative world more inclusive, which is largely influenced by the accessibility of languages imbedded in the programs. I can personally relate to the content of this reading: as a bilingual myself, I oftentimes appreciate the options of using different languages while using applications. Language accessibility is a crucial part of the user experience, and it's important as designers to recognize that and try to incorporate as many accessible functions as possible.
